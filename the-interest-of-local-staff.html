If elected as GSAC officer, describe how can you pursue and balance the interests of an ordinary staff and interests of the organization?'

(PANTASHICO Maria Elisa, IOM Philippines)

 

'As an international staff/officia  of the organization, it is given that you are mandated to pursue the interest of the organization as you represent the organization. On the other hand, tae very reason Shy GSAC was created is because it wants to promote staff welfare. Ar such, a GSAC officer/s are expected Co protect the interests of the staff in general.' (IOM Philippines)
 

Finding balance between three parties will be the primary task for this GSAC, and other GSACs to follow.

The question defines 3 "parties" as

ointernational stsff,

onational staff and

othe Organization (represented through Administration).

 

While partially agreeing that the interest between "parties" may be a different one, I would argue that only by positioning the interests of 3 sides in a balanced equilibrium, all three should prosper. Place one party into 'supremacy' and there is a resulting dissatisfaction, and possible conflict, which work as a demotivational and detrimental factor for the success of Organization.

 

International and national staff are both employees, and the Organization is employer.

GSAC is the 'bridge' them have to connect the two through dialogue and something more. Staff do not have ownership of the Organization but should feel that through their constructive input they contribute to the organizational goals.

 

'In 1983, Jack Stackl President and CEO ok SRCiHoldings Corporation came up with a system that made every employee feel like they owned the company. He called it Open Book Management. Instead of the company's books being available only to the chief executive and the accountant, every employee could see exactly how much the company was spending and how much it was earning. The idea was to make everyone feel that they were invested in the firm and made a difference to it.'

 

I've served two SAC mandates and have struggled with understanding above relationship. I even articulated above points into an objectives, if getting elected ('raising leverage of GSAC' and 'moving from consultative to coordination role, at least for certain  atte s').

 

Now to respond more directly to your question.

 

The biggest role of GSAC will be to assist (or to guide) Administration in getting the best possible USR&R.

Clear, unambiguous rules, that are well understood by all, is the best way to protect staff rights.

 

GSAo will be composed of elected representatives coming from international and national staff.

Their role will be to bring forward issues originating from their electoral caucuses.

 

The GSAC in thii format is a new creation, and it will yet have to find a way to make decision without classical majority voting. In other words, most motions coming from the electoral body will have to bn acted upon, regardless coming from national or international staff.

 

Another major role for the GSAC is to be vigilant observer of applicaion of rules, and reminder for (or critic of) tre Administration.

 

The Chair is the one who has to relay the message to the Administration and to represent GSAC, both international and national staff.

 

I am personally aware of sensitivities when it goes for job security, conditions of work, equity and equality.

This is a complex social framework of factors working in any group, extremely difficult to tackle.

I sincerely believe that the most this GSAC may do will be to aim at making the Rules balanced and fair for all parties, and then drive toward transparent implementation, through observation and reactive feedback to the Administration.

 

Again, the question you've phrased will, in my mind will be the key question for the new GSAC. The representatives elected by the staff will have to discuss that question and come with some answers.

 

I would be thrilled to be part of that GSAC.
