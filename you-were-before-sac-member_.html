You were before SAC member?

Q: Henry

"Hello Goran, You say communication is key, and it will be different when you are Chair.

Yet wou have been in the SAC twice, so what prevented you from doing that "better" communication when in the SAC?

This term the SAC News were a great improvement: were you leading this?"

 

A: Yes, I was serving 2 SAC tours when it was not a paid duty (I believe I am the only one among current candidates with a history of that kind of volunteering).

 

All SAC crews were recognizing that the communication is indeed the key issue: Apart from achieving better understanding, it has the ability to mobilize collective power and release new ideas. All SACs were and are doing their best. However, all of us were volunteers, struggling with their own professional duties and priorities.

 

Back in 2001 I was a "greenhorn" professional and can recall that our time and energy went mostly on a number of meetings (e.g. APBs, JARBs, weekly SAC meetings, etc). In the most recent SAC assemblies, the dedicated SAC secretary (paid by the Administration) made certain improvements possible.

 

No, I cannot attribute current SAC News to myself. I believe that we've inherited it from previous SAC crews and plan (if elected) to continue with it.

 

However, I personally count on explosion of social networking. I expect that this could give us better and accessible technical means to outreach to the staff. For this campaign, all candidates had to explore and move boundaries of their comfort (e.g. Yammer, web-sites, posters, etc). Solely this exercise is giving reasons for optimism.

 

My vision is to further explore SharePoint, Twitter and Teams, as well as creating some kind of repositories of discussion groups (e.g. if you are reading this text with a number of Q&As addressed by me, then you can imagine how technically GSAC discussions can be collated and published).

 

Another significant change I envisage is in the area of content (or what is published).

So far, SACs were playing honorable game with the Administration - SAC did not broadcast moments of and reasons for frustrations, when interventions to the Administration were not accepted. I believe that the GSAC needs to go one step further and to broadcast its own input and contribution. Administration can decide on their own, but at least positions will be transparent.
